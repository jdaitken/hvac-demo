Ottawa summers have been getting hotter, with temperatures regularly hitting 30°C and beyond. Your air conditioning system works hard during those July and August heat waves, so a little preventative maintenance goes a long way.

Here's a simple checklist to keep your AC running efficiently and avoid a breakdown on the hottest day of the year.

## DIY Maintenance (Monthly During Summer)

### Change or Clean Your Air Filter

This is the single most important thing you can do for your AC system. A dirty filter:

- **Restricts airflow**, making your system work harder
- **Increases energy costs** by 5-15%
- **Reduces air quality** in your home
- **Can lead to frozen evaporator coils**

Check your filter monthly and replace it at least every 3 months during heavy use.

### Clear Debris From the Outdoor Unit

Your condenser unit (the big box outside) needs clear airflow to work properly.

- Remove leaves, grass clippings, and debris from around the unit
- Keep at least **2 feet of clearance** on all sides
- Gently rinse the fins with a garden hose (never use a pressure washer)

### Check Your Thermostat Settings

- Make sure your thermostat is set to "cool" and "auto" (not "on")
- Consider upgrading to a programmable or smart thermostat — they can save you **10-15% on cooling costs**
- Set it to 25°C when you're home and 28°C when you're away

## Professional Maintenance (Annual — Book in Spring)

Some tasks require a licensed HVAC technician. We recommend booking your annual tune-up in **April or May**, before the summer rush.

### What a Professional Tune-Up Includes

- **Refrigerant level check** — low refrigerant means poor cooling and higher bills
- **Electrical connection inspection** — loose connections can be a fire hazard
- **Condenser and evaporator coil cleaning** — dirty coils reduce efficiency by up to 30%
- **Drain line clearing** — clogged drain lines can cause water damage
- **Blower motor inspection** — ensuring proper airflow throughout your home
- **Thermostat calibration** — making sure your home reaches the set temperature accurately

## Signs Your AC Needs Attention

Don't wait for a complete breakdown. Call a professional if you notice:

- **Warm air** coming from your vents when the AC is on
- **Unusual noises** like grinding, squealing, or banging
- **Higher-than-normal energy bills**
- **Frequent cycling** (turning on and off repeatedly)
- **Moisture or leaking** around the unit
- **Unpleasant odors** when the system runs

## The Ottawa Factor

Ottawa's freeze-thaw cycles and humid summers are particularly tough on AC systems. Spring pollen and cottonwood fluff can clog outdoor units quickly, and the transition from heating to cooling season means your system sits idle for months before being asked to perform at full capacity.

That's why a professional spring tune-up is especially important here — it catches issues that developed over the winter before they become expensive summer problems.
