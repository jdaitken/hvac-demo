Choosing the right HVAC contractor is one of the most important decisions you'll make as an Ottawa homeowner. A good contractor keeps your family comfortable and safe. A bad one can cost you thousands in botched work, voided warranties, and premature equipment failure.

Here's what to look for — and what to avoid.

## Must-Have Qualifications

### TSSA License

In Ontario, anyone working on gas appliances (furnaces, water heaters, fireplaces) must hold a valid **Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA)** license. This is non-negotiable.

**Ask to see it.** A legitimate contractor will gladly show you their credentials. You can also [verify licenses on the TSSA website](https://www.tssa.org).

### Proper Insurance

Your contractor should carry:

- **General liability insurance** (minimum $2 million)
- **WSIB (Workplace Safety and Insurance Board) coverage**

If an uninsured worker is injured on your property, *you* could be held liable. Always ask for proof of insurance.

### 313A Certification (for AC Work)

For air conditioning and refrigeration work, technicians need an Ontario **313A Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Systems Mechanic** certificate. This ensures they're qualified to handle refrigerants safely and properly.

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Unusually Low Quotes

If one quote is significantly lower than others, be cautious. Common shortcuts include:

- **Undersizing equipment** (cheaper but won't heat/cool effectively)
- **Skipping permits** (which can void insurance and create safety hazards)
- **Using inferior materials** (saving money now, costing you later)

A proper HVAC installation requires a **Manual J load calculation** to size equipment correctly for your home. If a contractor quotes without measuring your home, that's a red flag.

### High-Pressure Sales Tactics

Beware of contractors who:

- Insist you sign a contract immediately
- Won't provide a written quote
- Try to upsell equipment you don't need
- Use scare tactics about your current system

A good contractor educates you and gives you time to decide.

### No Written Contract

Always get a detailed, written contract that includes:

- Exact equipment make and models
- Total cost (including all labour and materials)
- Timeline for completion
- Warranty details (manufacturer AND labour)
- Permit responsibilities

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

1. **How long have you been in business in Ottawa?** Local experience matters — Ottawa's climate and housing stock have specific requirements.
2. **Can you provide references from recent Ottawa customers?**
3. **Do you pull permits for the work?** The answer should always be yes for installations.
4. **What brands do you install, and why?** A good contractor can explain their recommendations.
5. **What does your warranty cover?** Look for at least 1 year on labour in addition to manufacturer warranties.
6. **Do you offer maintenance plans?** Ongoing maintenance is essential for system longevity.

## The Value of Local

Ottawa's HVAC needs are unique. Our winters are among the coldest of any major Canadian city, and our summers are increasingly hot and humid. A contractor who knows Ottawa understands:

- The importance of properly sized heating equipment for -30°C nights
- Local building codes and permit requirements
- Which equipment performs best in our climate
- How to handle common Ottawa housing types (century homes, war-time bungalows, modern builds)
