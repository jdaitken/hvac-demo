Ottawa winters are no joke. When temperatures regularly dip to -20°C and below, a reliable furnace isn't a luxury — it's a necessity. But how do you know when it's time to stop repairing your old furnace and invest in a new one?

## The Average Furnace Lifespan

A well-maintained gas furnace typically lasts **15 to 20 years**. If your furnace is approaching or past this range, it's worth having a professional assess its condition. Even if it's still running, older furnaces become less efficient and more prone to costly breakdowns.

## Warning Signs Your Furnace Needs Replacing

### 1. Rising Energy Bills

If your heating bills are climbing each year despite similar usage, your furnace is likely losing efficiency. Modern high-efficiency furnaces (96%+ AFUE) can cut your heating costs by 30-40% compared to an older 60-70% efficient model.

### 2. Frequent Repairs

A good rule of thumb: if repair costs exceed **50% of the cost of a new furnace**, it's time to replace. Frequent breakdowns also mean you're at risk of being without heat when you need it most.

### 3. Uneven Heating

Cold spots in your home, rooms that won't warm up, or your thermostat constantly cycling on and off are signs your furnace can no longer heat your home effectively.

### 4. Strange Noises

Banging, rattling, squealing, or popping sounds often indicate worn-out components. While some noises are repairable, persistent or worsening sounds usually mean the system is failing.

### 5. Yellow Burner Flame

A healthy furnace flame is blue. A yellow or flickering flame can indicate a carbon monoxide issue — this requires **immediate professional attention**.

## Why Ottawa Homeowners Should Plan Ahead

The worst time to buy a new furnace is during an Ottawa cold snap when your old one has just died. You'll be under pressure to make a quick decision, and emergency installation during peak season can cost more.

**Our recommendation:** Have your furnace inspected each fall. If it's showing any of the above signs and is over 12 years old, start planning for a replacement before winter hits.

## The Cost of Waiting

A failing furnace doesn't just risk your comfort — it risks your safety. Cracked heat exchangers can leak carbon monoxide, and a furnace that dies on a -30°C night can lead to frozen pipes and thousands of dollars in water damage.
